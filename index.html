Course Description

Students learn history and theory of robots as communication and how they present novel and effective modes of public engagement, persuasion, and user-experience. Students research, plan, and execute automated communication projects in the form of social media bots, chatbots, and voice and text-based conversational interfaces. The course presumes no prior knowledge of programming. Students who enter with some programming knowledge, will have the opportunity to complete ‘stretch’ assignments.

Outcomes

Upon completion of the course students will be able to:

  • Identify different types of automated communication experiences that exist in the current marketplace
  • Describe the history of automated technologies as regarding effective contemporary communication
  • Demonstrate awareness of the ethical and legal implications of automated messaging
  • Analyze potential audiences and personas for a given instance of automated messaging
  • Systematically design and produce an automated communication experience
  • Assess the effectiveness of a given automated communication experience
